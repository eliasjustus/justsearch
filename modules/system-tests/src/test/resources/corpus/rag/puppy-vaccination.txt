Puppy Vaccination Schedule and Health Guide

Vaccinations are critical for protecting puppies from potentially fatal diseases. A complete
vaccination program follows a specific schedule based on the puppy's age.

Core Vaccines (Required):
1. DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parainfluenza, Parvovirus):
   - First dose: 6-8 weeks of age
   - Second dose: 10-12 weeks of age
   - Third dose: 14-16 weeks of age
   - Booster: 1 year after last puppy dose, then every 3 years

2. Rabies:
   - First dose: 12-16 weeks of age (required by law in most jurisdictions)
   - Booster: 1 year after first dose, then every 1-3 years depending on local law
   - The rabies vaccine is the only pet vaccine required by law in all 50 US states

Non-Core Vaccines (Based on Risk):
- Bordetella (kennel cough): Recommended for dogs in group settings like daycare or
  boarding. Given as early as 8 weeks, with boosters every 6-12 months.
- Leptospirosis: Recommended for dogs exposed to wildlife or standing water. Initial
  series of 2 doses 2-4 weeks apart, starting at 12 weeks.
- Lyme disease: Recommended in tick-endemic areas. Two-dose series starting at 12 weeks.
- Canine influenza (H3N2/H3N8): Two-dose series for dogs in group settings.

Important Guidelines:
- Puppies should NOT visit public areas or interact with unvaccinated dogs until 2 weeks
  after their final DHPP booster (around 18 weeks of age).
- Maternal antibodies from the mother's milk can interfere with vaccine effectiveness,
  which is why multiple doses are needed during the puppy series.
- Side effects are generally mild: lethargy, reduced appetite, slight fever for 24-48 hours.
  Severe reactions (facial swelling, vomiting, collapse) are rare but require immediate
  veterinary attention.
- The cost of a complete puppy vaccination series ranges from 75 to 200 dollars depending
  on the veterinary clinic and geographic location.
