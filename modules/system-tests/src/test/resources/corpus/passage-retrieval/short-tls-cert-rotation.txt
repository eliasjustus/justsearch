TLS Certificate Rotation Best Practices for Production Services

TLS certificate rotation is a critical operational practice for maintaining secure encrypted
communications without service interruptions. Certificates have finite validity periods and must
be renewed before expiration to prevent outages.

Key certificate rotation strategies include:

Automated renewal: Use certificate management tools (cert-manager, ACME clients, or cloud
provider integrations) to automatically request new certificates before the current ones expire.
Target renewal at 2/3 of the certificate lifetime (e.g., renew 60-day certificates after 40
days).

Graceful rollover: Deploy new certificates alongside old ones during the transition period.
Servers should accept both the old and new certificates until all clients have picked up the
change. This overlap window prevents connection failures during rotation.

Health monitoring: Continuously monitor certificate expiration dates across all services and
endpoints. Alert when certificates are within 30 days of expiration. Track the entire certificate
chain including intermediate and root certificates.

Secret distribution: After obtaining a renewed certificate, update the secret store and trigger
a reload in consuming services. Use file-watching or signal-based reloading to pick up new
certificates without restarting the service process.

Chain validation: Verify that the full certificate chain is valid after rotation. Test that
clients can verify the chain from the leaf certificate through intermediates to the trusted root.
Missing intermediate certificates are a common cause of rotation failures.

Audit logging: Record all certificate issuance, renewal, and revocation events. Maintain an
inventory of all certificates with their subjects, issuers, serial numbers, and expiration dates
for compliance and incident response.
