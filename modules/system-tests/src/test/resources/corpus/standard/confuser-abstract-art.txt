Abstract Art History and Movements

Abstract art represents a departure from reality in depiction of imagery in art.
The movement emerged in the early 20th century and fundamentally changed how we 
think about visual expression.

Key Movements:
- Cubism (1907-1920s): Picasso, Braque - geometric fragmentation
- Suprematism (1913): Malevich - pure geometric forms
- De Stijl (1917): Mondrian - primary colors and straight lines
- Abstract Expressionism (1940s-1950s): Pollock, Rothko

Characteristics:
- Non-representational imagery
- Focus on color, form, line, and texture
- Emotional rather than visual representation
- Freedom from traditional artistic constraints

Famous Works:
- "Composition VIII" - Wassily Kandinsky (1923)
- "Black Square" - Kazimir Malevich (1915)
- "No. 61 (Rust and Blue)" - Mark Rothko (1953)
