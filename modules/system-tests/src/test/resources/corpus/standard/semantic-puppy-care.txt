Welcoming a New Puppy Home

Bringing a young pup into your family is an exciting time. Proper preparation ensures 
a smooth transition for both you and your new furry family member.

Essential Supplies:
- Food and water bowls (stainless steel recommended)
- High-quality puppy food appropriate for the breed size
- Comfortable crate for training and safe sleeping
- Collar, leash, and ID tags
- Toys for mental stimulation and teething

Socialization Period:
The first 12-16 weeks are critical for socialization. Expose your young companion to:
- Different people of all ages
- Other vaccinated, friendly pets
- Various sounds, surfaces, and environments
- Car rides and visits to pet-friendly stores

Health Care:
Schedule a veterinary visit within the first week. Discuss vaccination schedules,
parasite prevention, spaying/neutering, and nutrition. Puppies need multiple rounds
of vaccinations before they're fully protected.

House Training:
Take your little one outside frequently - after meals, naps, and play sessions.
Reward outdoor elimination enthusiastically. Never punish accidents; just clean
thoroughly with enzyme cleaner to remove scent markers.
