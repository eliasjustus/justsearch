# JustSearch Individual Contributor License Agreement (ICLA)

> **Plain-language summary (not the legal text — the agreement below governs).**
> You **keep the copyright** to everything you contribute. By signing, you give the JustSearch
> maintainer a broad licence to use your contribution — including the right to license the project
> (as a whole) under other terms in the future, e.g. an additional commercial licence alongside the
> open-source one. This is **not** an assignment and it does **not** make JustSearch proprietary:
> the project is and remains open-source under **Apache-2.0**, and your contribution stays available
> under that licence too. The CLA simply keeps the project's long-term licensing options open. You
> sign it **once** (a bot handles it on your first pull request); it covers all your future
> contributions. If you contribute on behalf of an employer, see §8.
>
> *Why a CLA at all?* With only the open-source licence, the project could never offer a
> dual/commercial licence later without tracking down and getting consent from every past
> contributor — which becomes impossible. The CLA preserves that option now, while contributors are
> few, without taking anything away from you.

## How this is signed

You do **not** email or post this document. When you open your **first pull request**, an automated
**CLA assistant** comments on the PR; you confirm your agreement by replying once (a single comment),
which records your signature against your GitHub account. After that, all your future pull requests
are covered automatically. Until the CLA is signed, a PR cannot be merged.

The CLA is the canonical contributor agreement for JustSearch. Normal pull requests do not require
a separate per-commit sign-off.
