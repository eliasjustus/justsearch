# The Clockwork Garden of Anselm Vey

Anselm Vey spent thirty years building the Clockwork Garden in the town of
Little Whimbrel. Every flower was a brass mechanism that opened at dawn and
folded shut at dusk, driven by a buried mainspring the size of a millstone. The
garden's centrepiece was a mechanical heron that struck the hour by dipping its
beak into a pond of mercury. When Vey died in 1934, the mainspring was wound one
last time and the garden ran, untended, for a further nine years.
